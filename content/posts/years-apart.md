I recently looked back at one of my older projects and compared it to something I've built more recently. They don't just look different. They feel like they were built by completely different people.

## Then

**[Soto Breeding Roller](https://github.com/PSNapier/sotobreedingroller)** - a small commissioned breeding roller built with vanilla JavaScript and CSS.

I could build what I needed, but I hadn't figured out _how_ I wanted to build things yet. No consistent structure, no separation of concerns. If I needed to add genes, I worked them directly into the logic. If something broke, I dug through the code until I found where it lived. It worked, but it wasn't designed. No gene dictionaries, no reusable systems. just a tangle that made sense because I was the one who wrote it.

And the CSS? Let's say I'm glad Tailwind exists.

## Now

**[Rattlesnake Mountain](https://github.com/PSNapier/rattlesnake-mountain)** - a full ARPG site built with Laravel and Tailwind, with user accounts, custom admin tools, and a shop populated from external data.

The difference isn't scope, it's approach. Logic is reusable by default, data is separated and easy to extend, features are planned around how they'll grow, and admin usability is part of the design. A lot of what started in simple rollers (handling complex rules, edge cases, and outputs) translated directly into building better internal tools.

## What Progress Actually Looks Like

Early on, development felt like trying to understand something in the dark (something something that parable about a bunch of blind men and an elephant). Every piece made sense on its own, but nothing quite connected. Now most problems have a recognizable shape. I know where to start, what layer is involved, what questions to ask. Less trial-and-error, more pattern recognition, not because things got simpler, but because I've built enough to see how they fit together.

The difference between these two projects isn't talent. It's repetition. Building things the wrong way, noticing where they break, doing it better next time. There's no moment where everything clicks. The lights just come on, gradually.

If you're still in the phase where everything feels messy and disconnected, that's normal. That's what this looks like before it starts to make sense.
